QIP: Switching of Eye Drops

Objectives

Switching all adult patients to Eyeaz 0.1% or 0.2% who are prescribed the following:

  • Hylo-Tear 0.1% eye drops
  • Hy-Opti 0.1% eye drops
  • Xailin Tears HA 0.1% eye drops
  • Hylo-Forte 0.2% eye drops
  • Hy-Opti 0.2% eye drops
  • Clintas Multi 0.2% eye drops
  • Evolve HA 0.2% eye drops
  • Xailin Plus HA 0.2% eye drops
  • HydraMed 0.2% eye drops

Switch applies to 0.1% and 0.2% strengths of the branded generics available. Switching Sodium Hyaluronate 0.15% eye drops, however, reamins the prescriber's clinical decision to switch to either 0.1% or 0.2% product, if clinically appropariate to do so.

Background

For patients who have a diagnosis of dry eyes, Sodiium Hyaluronate eye drops, also known as artificial tears, are prescribed. The eye drops are used to relieve dry eyes and soreness, lubricating the surface of the eye to increase comfort.

As per NICE CKS guidance, a prescription for treatment of dry or sore eyes should not routinely be offered in primary care as the condition is appropriate for self-care.
